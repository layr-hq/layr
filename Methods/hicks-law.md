# Hick’s Law

## What it is
The more options a user has, the longer it takes them to make a decision.

## Why it matters
Too many choices increases hesitation, confusion, and drop-off.

## How to apply
- limit visible options at any moment
- prioritise one primary action
- group related choices together
- use progressive disclosure (show more only when needed)

## Example
Bad:
10 buttons with equal weight

Good:
1 primary action + 1–2 secondary options

## Fail signs
- user pauses to decide
- user scans multiple times
- user doesn’t know what to click

## Rule
Reduce choices until the next step feels obvious.
