# Visual Hierarchy

## What it is
The order in which users perceive elements on a screen.

## Why it matters
Good hierarchy guides attention. Bad hierarchy causes confusion.

## How to apply
- use size, spacing, and weight to prioritise
- make primary actions stand out
- reduce emphasis on less important elements

## Example
Bad:
Everything looks equally important

Good:
Clear primary → secondary → tertiary flow

## Fail signs
- user doesn’t know where to look
- important actions are missed

## Rule
Make the most important thing impossible to miss.
