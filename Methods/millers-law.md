# Miller’s Law

## What it is
People can hold about 7 (±2) items in working memory.

## Why it matters
Too much information at once overwhelms users.

## How to apply
- limit visible items in a group
- break content into smaller chunks
- use grouping and hierarchy
- avoid long lists without structure

## Example
Bad:
12+ options shown at once

Good:
Grouped into smaller, digestible sections

## Fail signs
- user feels overwhelmed
- user struggles to process choices

## Rule
Chunk information into manageable groups.
