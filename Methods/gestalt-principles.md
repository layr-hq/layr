# Gestalt Principles

## What it is
How humans naturally group and interpret visual information.

Key principles:
- Proximity (things close together feel related)
- Similarity (things that look alike feel related)
- Continuity (we follow visual paths)
- Figure/Ground (we separate foreground from background)

## Why it matters
Poor grouping = confusion. Good grouping = instant understanding.

## How to apply
- group related items closely
- separate unrelated items with space
- keep consistent styles for related elements
- make primary content clearly stand out

## Example
Bad:
Everything evenly spaced, no grouping

Good:
Clear sections with grouped content

## Fail signs
- user can’t tell what belongs together
- layout feels messy or random

## Rule
Design so structure is understood instantly without reading.
