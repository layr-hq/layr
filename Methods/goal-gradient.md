# Goal Gradient Effect

## What
The Goal Gradient Effect states that users accelerate their effort and motivation as they get closer to completing a goal.

Visible progress increases the likelihood of completion.

## Why it matters
Users are more likely to continue and finish a task when they can see how close they are to the end.

Lack of progress visibility leads to:
- drop-off mid-flow
- reduced motivation
- uncertainty about completion

Clear progress creates:
- momentum
- motivation
- higher completion rates

## When to apply
- onboarding flows
- multi-step forms
- checkout processes
- setup and configuration flows
- any process with more than one step

## How to apply

- show clear progress indicators (steps, bars, percentages)
- break tasks into smaller, visible stages
- communicate how much is completed and what remains
- make progress feel achievable and close
- highlight completion milestones

Users should always feel like they are moving forward.

## Implementation rules

- always show progress for multi-step flows
- keep total steps low (ideally 3–5 steps)
- clearly label current step and next step
- update progress immediately after each action
- avoid long, undefined processes with no visible end
- show completion state clearly at the end

## Example

Bad:
- long process with no indication of progress
- user unsure how much is left
- no sense of advancement

Good:
- step-based flow (Step 2 of 4)
- visible progress bar
- clear next step and completion state

## Fail conditions

- user drops off before completion
- user unsure how far they are in the process
- user feels the task is too long or unclear
- no visible sense of progress

## Enforcement rule

If the user cannot see progress toward completion, add clear progress indicators until advancement feels obvious and motivating.
